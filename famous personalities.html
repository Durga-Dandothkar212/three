Famous Personalities

Nick Vujicic

Summary

Nick Vujicic is an Australian preacher and motivational speaker. Nicholas James Vujicic was born on 4th December 1982 with Tetra-amelia syndrome, a rare disorder characterized by the absence of all four limbs.

Achievements

Vujicic was felicitated with the “Australian Young Citizen Award” for his determination and courage in 1990. In 2009, Vujicic got featured in a short film titled 'The Butterfly Circus', directed by Joshua Weigel in 2009. The film also starred Eduardo Verástegui, a Mexican actor, and American Doug Jones.

Inspiration

Nick Vujicic's life was a constant battle of self-acceptance and grief, but his mother made him realize that his imperfection is a perfect inspiration to others. It was his life's turning point where his mother showed him a piece of article from a newspaper about a man who's still happy even with disability.

Sarvepally Radhakrishnan

summary

Sarvepally Radhakrishnan was born on September 5, 1888, in Tiruttani, Tamilnadu. He was an eminent philosopher, scholar, and one of India's most distinguished twentieth-century thinkers. Dr. Radhakrishnan served as the second President of India from 1962 to 1967.

Achievements

Radhakrishnan was awarded several high awards during his life, including a knighthood in 1931, the Bharat Ratna, the highest civilian award in India, in 1954, and honorary membership of the British Royal Order of Merit in 1963.

Inspiration

He was not only a renowned teacher and philosopher but also the second President of independent India. Dr. Radhakrishnan believed in the transformative power of education and its role in shaping society. He bridged the gap between Eastern and Western philosophies and authored several books on philosophy and religion.

Latha Mangeshkar

Summary

Lata Mangeshkar was born in Indore on September 28, 1929, and became, quite simply, the most popular playback singer in Bollywood's history. She sung for over 50 years for actresses from Nargis to Preity G Zinta, as well as recorded albums of all kinds (ghazals, pop, etc).

Achievements

Mangeshkar won several awards and honours, including the Bharat Ratna (2001), India's highest civilian award, Padma Bhushan (1969), Padma Vibhushan (1999), Zee Cine Award for Lifetime Achievements (1999), Dadasaheb Phalke Award (1989), Maharashtra Bhushan Award (1997), NTR National Award (1999), Legion of Honour (2007)

Inspiration

Lata Mangeshkar was a woman of determination, willpower, and commitment. She was hardworking and devoted to her work. She convinced her father to give her wings so she could fly.
